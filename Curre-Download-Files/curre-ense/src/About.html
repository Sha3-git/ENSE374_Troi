Curre is a company dedicated to increasing the accessibility of fitness for all individuals.

Background


We have observed that there is an overwhelming amount of considerations to be made before the purchase of running shoes. Our goal with this endeavour is to provide newomers to running with the right tools and education to have a more delightful and impactful experience.

Project Goals

Business Need

Mission: We’re in business to inspire more delightful and impactful running experiences by connecting people to the right tools.
Vision: To create a better running experience for everyone

Our Enablers

Purpose: Building better running experiences through a customized footwear assessment system.
Values: Accessiblity, Education, Diversity, Inspiration
Goals: Reduce the roadblock to novice runners, provide an accessible place to start their research, and generate a personalized footwear recommendation software

Strategic Focus Areas

Lifestyle: We hope to encourage people to adopt a healthy lifestyle in their daily routine by removing the technical roadblocks.
Education: We seek to break down the intimidating barricades to running by providing the means to accessible knowledge and sources.
Sport: We will focus on the most common sport category: running. Running and walking take the least amount of equipment, space, organization, and interdependence on several factors.

More About Shoe Considerations

Terrain
The type of terrain one runs in greatly affects the type of shoe needed for that environment. The shoe required will need different types of grip and balance for the both the safety and comfort of the runner.

Cushioning
Cushioning in a shoe is its capacity to absorb the force of an impact. So, the function of a shoe’s cushioning is to make the contact phase as gradual as possible in order to minimize the inevitable impact with the ground.

Heel Drop
Heel drop is essentially a measurement for how fat a running shoe’s heel feels. It’s short for “heel to toe drop,” often shortened to just “drop.” It’s often called “offset,” and sometimes called a “ramp angle.”

Pronation
Pronation is part of the natural movement of the human body and refers to the way your foot rolls inward for impact distribution upon landing. Understanding your pronation can help you find a comfortable running shoe.

9 mins

Shoe Width
Shoe width plays an important role in the comfort and health of your feet. Wearing shoes that are too tight can create pressure and friction on your feet and lead to many foot problems.
